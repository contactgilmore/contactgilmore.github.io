Hey, I'm Mike Gilmore.

This is where I share what I’m building, automating, and learning across cloud architecture, observability, DevOps tooling, and reliability engineering. Most of it comes from real projects, production lessons, and the occasional deep dive into something that annoyed me enough to fix.

You’ll find write-ups on infrastructure decisions, monitoring strategies, automation workflows, and the tradeoffs behind them. I care about systems that are reliable, understandable, and efficient, and I’m big on documentation that actually helps the next person.

This site is part of that process. It’s built with Jekyll plus custom HTML and CSS. I was skeptical at first, but once I got the structure dialed in, it turned into a solid way to publish work without fighting the tooling.

I’m always updating this with projects and experiments, mostly built locally in my free time. The blog is where I go deeper and capture the lessons and decisions behind the work.

About Me

I build and support systems that hold up under pressure. Production environments are rarely predictable, and I enjoy the challenge of designing infrastructure that stays reliable when things get messy. My work spans cloud engineering, DevOps, and site reliability, with a strong focus on observability, automation, and infrastructure that is actually maintainable.

Before moving fully into engineering, I led teams in retail operations. That experience shaped how I work today. I value clear communication, steady decision making, and staying calm when stakes are high. Technical depth matters, but so does making sure the people around you understand the system and can operate it confidently.

Over the years, I have helped teams improve onboarding, clean up documentation, automate repetitive tasks, and dig through incidents until the root cause was clear. I enjoy connecting the dots between systems, teams, and processes. The goal is always the same: reduce noise, increase clarity, and leave things better than I found them.

I am motivated by steady improvement. Whether it is refining an alerting strategy, simplifying an infrastructure deployment, or helping a teammate think through a problem, I care about building systems that are reliable and people can trust.
